Support Policy

We only offer support for our Managed Spica customers, customers paying for hourly support. However, you can ask for help on our Slack or Discord community if you are using open source licenses!

For issues with Spica itself, chances are submitting a Bug Report is the best course or action. If you believe you have found an issue with the Spica, API, SDK, or Documentation â€” please head over to GitHub to fill out a Bug Report on the appropriate repository.

Support Options

The resources below are available to anyone with high-level sales, product, or strategic questions, but only Managed Spica customers or on-premise licensed customers can request support. Response times vary depending on support method, SLA details, and time of day, but basic support is available from 10am to 5pm GMT+3, Monday through Friday.

Not Supported

You'll likely have a few questions along the way, and while we would love to help find solutions for everyone, we need to be realistic with the scope of our support. Below are a few topics that we unfortunately can't help with.

  • Issues with your project's front-end
  • Self-hosted and local projects
  • Alpha and Beta features
  • Third-party integrations
  • Analyzing logs
